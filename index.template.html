Method

Conditions = cross-source consensus of open slicer + manufacturer data (OFD·MIT, Orca/Prusa/Bambu·AGPL, Cura·CC0). Temperature is a range — strength picks the max (higher nozzle temp → stronger layer bond; ~+20 °C headroom above optimal before quality fails, per 948k CAXTON frames). Not measured optima; single-source materials are flagged, and cross-confirmed ones show their source spread.

Disclaimer

Values are aggregated consensus suggestions, provided AS IS with no warranty — a starting point, not a guarantee. Always run a temperature tower / calibration print, and never exceed your filament's or hotend's rated limits. You are responsible for the settings you apply; ParaMat is not liable for failed prints or equipment damage.

Max volumetric flow is also a hotend limit. Verify with a quick extrude-in-air test at rising rates: the strand should coil out soft (fully melted); if it comes out stiff/straight you're past the melt capacity — slow down, run hotter, or fit a high-flow hotend. Method: Ellis' Print Tuning Guide.
