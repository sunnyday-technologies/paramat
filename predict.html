How this works. A physics anchor (material identity × load-bearing fraction from infill/walls/flow × orientation × thermal bonding, Gibson–Ashby cellular scaling) corrected by a ridge model trained on 102 published measured coupons, with conformal 90% intervals. Intervals are infill-conditional: sparse parts genuinely scatter more, so their bands are honestly wider. Predictions are referenced to a standard 13 × 3.2 mm tensile coupon (ASTM D638 Type I) with slicer-default shell (selected walls + 0.8 mm solid top/bottom skins; walls = 0 means a bare laminate) — real part geometry shifts wall share.

Walls enter the load model (0.48 mm line each); infill pattern is not yet modeled — no open dataset states pattern AND density together. If you have such data, we want it.

Honest limits. Cross-study error on held-out studies is 1–13 MPa depending on how completely the process is specified; the interval, not the point, is the product. Model + data: open source, predictions CC BY 4.0.
